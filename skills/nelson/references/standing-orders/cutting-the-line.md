# Standing Order: Cutting the Line

When a low-priority blocker holds up high-value work, a captain may request permission to proceed in parallel.

**When to Signal:**
- A blocking dependency is low priority but the dependent task is high value or time-critical.
- The rework risk from proceeding without the blocker is bounded and understood.
- Waiting would waste significant budget or miss the deadline.

**Procedure:** Captain signals the admiral with a risk assessment: what could change, what rework would be needed, and the cost of waiting vs. proceeding. Admiral approves or denies. If approved, red-cell navigator reviews the risk assessment. Captain proceeds with explicit acknowledgement that rework may be required when the blocker eventually completes.
